— A reading room for a war —

The legal record
of this war.

Forty-one international legal proceedings between Ukraine and the Russian Federation, indexed for reading. What was done. What was ruled. What is owed.

2014 — annexation  ·  2022 — invasion  ·  2026 — the present
I

What was done.

Act 01 / 03
The harms
298
people aboard MH17

Shot down by a Buk surface-to-air missile fired from a field near Pervomaiskyi, eastern Ukraine, on the 17th of July 2014.

19,500+
Ukrainian children

Identified as deported or forcibly transferred to the Russian Federation from occupied territories. The basis of the ICC's first warrant against a sitting P5 head of state.

12
years · territory occupied

Crimea has been under Russian control since February 2014. The Donbas since the spring of the same year. Most of the south since 2022.

on territory

The annexation that began everything.

In February 2014 Russian forces seized the Crimean peninsula. By March they had organised a referendum. By summer the Donbas was burning. The international legal cases that began that year — at the ECtHR, at the PCA, at the SCC arbitration in Stockholm — never ended; they multiplied.

By 2026 there are at least eleven distinct PCA arbitrations arising from Crimea alone, all under the 1998 Russia–Ukraine BIT. Together they form the largest single cluster of investor-state arbitration in the history of any treaty.

on the body

The missiles, the dam, the children.

The full-scale invasion of February 2022 brought new categories of harm into court: the missile campaign against the Ukrainian energy grid, the destruction of the Kakhovka hydroelectric dam, the seizure of the Zaporizhzhia nuclear plant, the deportation of children.

Six ICC arrest warrants flow from this period. Putin and Lvova-Belova for the children. Kobylash, Sokolov, Shoigu, Gerasimov for the strikes on civilian infrastructure. None of them, as of April 2026, has been served.

The first ICC warrant ever issued against a sitting permanent-five head of state.
— On Putin, March 2023
II

What was ruled.

Act 02 / 03
The judgments
3
major ICJ rulings

The 2024 CERD/ICSFT merits judgment. The 2022 provisional measures order in Genocide. The provisional measures in the Crimea case. The world court has not stood quiet.

7—0
unanimous violations · ECtHR

In the Crimea Grand Chamber judgment of June 2024, every article — 2, 3, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10, 11, 14, 18, P1-1, P1-2, P4-2 — was violated. Without a single dissent.

1st
Article 84 in 80 years

In May 2025, the ICAO Council made the first merits ruling under Article 84 of the Chicago Convention in its eighty-year history — finding Russia responsible for the downing of MH17.

the world court

Genocide, in reverse.

In Allegations of Genocide, Ukraine asks the ICJ to declare not that Russia committed genocide, but that its invocation of the Genocide Convention as pretext for war is itself a violation of that Convention.

It is one of the most legally inventive applications ever filed. Thirty-two states have intervened — the largest intervention in the Court's history.

the european court

The largest case in European history.

Ukraine and the Netherlands v. Russia bundles four inter-state applications — the Donbas, MH17, the post-2022 invasion, the abductions of children — into one Grand Chamber proceeding.

The merits judgment came down in July 2025. Russia is liable for the downing of MH17, among everything else.

the dutch supreme court

Six December, 2024.

On a single day, the Hoge Raad confirmed three of the four Crimea BIT awards Russia had challenged. The Naftogaz, PrivatBank, and Belbek awards stand. Only the Everest case — on a narrow evidentiary point — was remanded.

The most consequential set of rulings in international arbitration in a decade.

III

What is owed.

Act 03 / 03
The money
€210bn
frozen at Euroclear, Brussels

The single largest pool of Russian state money under European control. As of December 2025, frozen indefinitely. The reparations-loan structure remains under negotiation.

$5bn
awarded to Naftogaz, PCA

For the expropriation of the Naftogaz group's Crimean oil and gas assets. Final award rendered April 2023; confirmed by the Hoge Raad in December 2024; enforcement underway in five jurisdictions.

$1.37bn
awarded in Naftogaz v Gazprom (III)

The first major arbitral win triggered by the full-scale invasion. Final award June 2025; confirmed by the Swiss Federal Tribunal in March 2026.

Awarded does not mean collected.
— Estimated $300m physically attached, of $7.5bn awarded

Coda
Five things to remember
forums

Eleven different courtrooms

From the World Court at the Peace Palace to the Schiphol criminal courthouse. From the SCC in Stockholm to a Lithuanian district court in Vilnius. The legal record is dispersed across eleven kinds of bench.

enforcement

Five jurisdictions chasing assets

Naftogaz alone is now enforcing its Crimea award in France, the United Kingdom, Finland, Austria, and the United States. The Dutch Supreme Court, the Swiss Federal Tribunal, and the French Cour de cassation have all refused Russia's challenges.

unfinished

The cases not yet filed

Energoatom has put Russia on notice over the Zaporizhzhia plant. Ukrhydroenergo over Kakhovka. The Special Tribunal for the Crime of Aggression begins sitting in 2027. The legal record of this war is, by any measure, not yet written.
