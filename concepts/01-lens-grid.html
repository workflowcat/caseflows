Three things to know first.

a 90-second primer
before you click in
01
Many Hague courts

"The Hague" is not one building.

The ICJ hears disputes between states. The ICC prosecutes individuals. The PCA hosts arbitrations between investors and states. They share a city — and almost nothing else.

02
Three layers of harm

Territory. Civilians. Money.

The cases roughly split into three stories: territory (Crimea, Donbas, the Sea of Azov), civilians (MH17, mass killings, deportations of children), and money (gas contracts, expropriated assets, frozen reserves).

03
Enforcement is its own war

A judgment is not the end.

Many of the most consequential rulings come after a tribunal speaks: the Hoge Raad upholding an award, a French court issuing exequatur, a Finnish bailiff freezing assets. Enforcement is its own story.

Three big stories, many courtrooms.

About caseflows

A reader's guide to international legal proceedings between Ukraine and the Russian Federation, 2014–present. Each case has a structured page: parties, forum, dates, key rulings, plain-language summary.

Caseflows is a reader project, not a legal-advice service. Where we summarise a ruling, we link the underlying judgment.
