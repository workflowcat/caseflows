Before you click in: three things to know about international law.

01 · Many Hague courts "The Hague" is not one building. The ICJ hears disputes between states. The ICC prosecutes individuals. The PCA hosts arbitrations between investors and states. They share a city — and almost nothing else.
02 · Three layers of harm The cases roughly split into three stories: territory (Crimea, Donbas, the Sea of Azov), civilians (MH17, mass killings, deportations of children), and money (gas contracts, expropriated assets, frozen reserves).
03 · A judgment is not the end Many of the most consequential rulings come after a tribunal speaks: the Hoge Raad upholding an award, a French court issuing exequatur, a Finnish bailiff freezing assets. Enforcement is its own story.

About this database

Caseflows is a reader's guide to the international legal proceedings that have arisen between Ukraine and the Russian Federation since 2014. Each case has its own structured page: parties, forum, dates, key rulings, and a plain-language summary.

We track cases at international courts (ICJ, ECtHR, ICC, ITLOS, ICAO), arbitral tribunals (PCA, SCC, ICC), and national courts in the Netherlands, Switzerland, France, the UK, Finland, Lithuania and Germany.
